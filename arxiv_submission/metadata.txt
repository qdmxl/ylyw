abstract: >
  Current embodied intelligence decision-making methods predominantly rely on 
  data-driven deep learning paradigms, suffering from fundamental bottlenecks in 
  data efficiency, interpretability, and physical compliance. This paper proposes 
  YLYW (Yi-Li Inspired Physical World Modeling), a knowledge-driven third path 
  that formalizes the I Ching's 64-hexagram symbolic system as a prior knowledge 
  manual within a federated neuro-symbolic architecture.
  
  Our core innovations include: (1) treating the eight trigrams as continuous fuzzy 
  membership bases, resolving the symbol grounding problem; (2) proposing a systematic 
  hexagram template optimization method based on object yao-vector centroids; 
  (3) for the first time, formalizing the five yao-position relationships (proper 
  position, central position, override, support, adjacency harmony, resonance) from 
  the Zhou Yi as computable operators, establishing a hierarchical decision system 
  where the hexagram determines "what to do" and yao relations determine "how to do it."
  
  Under a full 64-hexagram rule base, the system achieves 92.7% strategy rationality 
  rate and 0% critical error rate on 300-object zero-shot baseline testing, with pure 
  prior reasoning requiring only 1.7ms per object. The performance leap from 48.0% to 
  92.7% itself serves as a vivid validation of the interpretability of the Yi-Li model.
